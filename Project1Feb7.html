Understanding Consensus in Distributed Systems

1. Problem statement

(What is a distributed system?) A distributed system is a network of computers working together to accomplish a task. Vatious computers or nodes in a distributed system have to agree on steps to be executed for a consistent and correct outcome for the operations in this system. Consistent operations of a distributed is often referred to as consensus and this is a universal problem in everyday systems such as democratic voting to elect a leader and in strategies for team operations. Consensus is about coordination and agreement on how to reach a consistant state for the system in presence of presence of failure and malicious nodes. In this project, we will emulate a distributed system using the light weight virtualization or container technology in Docker [1] and evaluate specic consensus algorithm. The main resource for learning about consensus is your text book [2] Chapter 15 and the references provided in this document. Now lets get started, shall we?
